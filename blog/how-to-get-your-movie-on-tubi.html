How to Get Your Movie on Tubi: A Filmmaker's Guide to the Fastest-Growing AVOD Platform

Tubi has quietly become one of the most important platforms in the independent film distribution landscape. With tens of millions of monthly active users and a massive content library, the free, ad-supported streaming service offers filmmakers a genuine opportunity to reach large audiences. If you're wondering how to get your movie on Tubi, here's what you need to know.

Why Tubi Matters for Independent Filmmakers

Tubi operates on an AVOD (advertising-based video on demand) model, which means viewers watch content for free and revenue is generated through ads. For filmmakers, this translates to earnings based on viewership volume. The more people who watch your film, the more revenue it generates.

What makes Tubi particularly attractive is its audience size and growth trajectory. The platform has experienced year-over-year growth that outpaces many of its competitors, and its user base spans a wide range of demographics. Fox Corporation's ownership of Tubi has also brought additional resources and visibility to the platform.

For independent filmmakers, Tubi represents a sweet spot. The platform actively acquires independent content across virtually every genre, and the barrier to entry is more accessible than platforms like Netflix or Hulu. That said, there is still a process involved, and understanding it is key to getting your film placed.

How Content Gets on Tubi

Tubi acquires content through licensing agreements, typically negotiated with distributors and content aggregators. Like most major platforms, Tubi does not generally accept direct uploads from individual filmmakers. The platform works with distribution partners who supply content that meets its quality standards and audience interests.

This means the most reliable path to getting your movie on Tubi is working with a distributor who has an existing relationship with the platform. A distributor handles the pitch, negotiates the licensing terms, manages technical delivery, and ensures your film meets all of Tubi's content and format requirements.

Octane Multimedia maintains a direct relationship with Tubi and regularly places independent titles on the platform. Working with an established distributor removes the logistical complexity and gives your film the best chance of acceptance.

What Tubi Looks For

Genre Fit

Tubi's audience gravitates toward certain genres more than others. Action, horror, thriller, comedy, and drama tend to perform particularly well on the platform. Family content and documentaries also have a solid presence. If your film falls within one of these categories, it's likely a natural fit for Tubi's catalog.

That said, Tubi's library is expansive, and the platform acquires across a broad range of genres. Don't assume your film won't work just because it falls outside the most popular categories.

Production Quality

While Tubi's quality threshold is more accessible than some premium subscription platforms, the content still needs to meet professional standards. Clean picture quality, a balanced audio mix, and proper formatting are all essential. Films that look and sound like professional productions will be received much more favorably than those with visible technical shortcomings.

Completed Deliverables

Tubi, like all platforms, requires specific technical deliverables. These include a high-quality video master, properly formatted audio, closed captions, metadata, and artwork that meets the platform's specifications. A distributor manages this entire delivery process, ensuring nothing falls through the cracks.

How Filmmakers Earn Revenue on Tubi

Tubi's AVOD model means your earnings are tied to viewership. The platform serves ads during content playback, and a portion of that ad revenue flows back to the content provider. The specific revenue arrangement depends on the terms negotiated between the distributor and the platform.

What makes Tubi compelling from a revenue standpoint is the potential for long-tail earnings. Unlike a one-time licensing fee, an AVOD model means your film continues to earn as long as people keep watching it. A title that resonates with Tubi's audience can generate steady income over months or even years.

Performance on Tubi can also have a compounding effect. Films that perform well get promoted more heavily by the platform's recommendation system, which drives additional viewership and additional revenue. Getting placed on Tubi is the first step, but strong initial performance can create a cycle of increasing visibility.

Positioning Tubi Within a Larger Distribution Strategy

Tubi should be one component of a broader distribution plan, not the only outlet for your film. A well-structured strategy might include an initial TVOD window on transactional platforms, followed by placement on AVOD services like Tubi alongside SVOD licensing deals where appropriate.
